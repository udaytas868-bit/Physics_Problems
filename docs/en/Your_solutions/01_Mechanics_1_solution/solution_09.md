## 9. Momentum Comparison: Step-by-Step Analysis

### Step 1: Define the Formula
Momentum ($p$) is defined as the product of mass ($m$) and velocity ($v$): $p = m \cdot v$.

### Step 2: Unit Conversion (Grams to Kilograms)
In SI units, mass must be in kilograms ($1 \text{ g} = 0.001 \text{ kg}$):
* **Fly mass ($m_1$):** $2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$
* **Tennis ball mass ($m_2$):** $60 \text{ g} = 0.060 \text{ kg}$

### Step 3: Calculate and Compare
* **Fly Momentum ($p_1$):** $0.002 \text{ kg} \cdot 10 \text{ m/s} = 0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$
* **Tennis Ball Momentum ($p_2$):** $0.060 \text{ kg} \cdot 1 \text{ m/s} = 0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$
**Final Result:** The tennis ball has a greater momentum ($0.06 > 0.02$).
